How I Think About Growth
Growth isn't a task for one stage — it's a continuous thread across a product's entire lifecycle. Each stage has its own core tension and demands a completely different playbook. Knowing where you are matters more than moving faster.
Prerequisite
The Perception Gap: Growth's Hidden Ceiling

Most products — especially early-stage ones — don't struggle with "not enough traffic." They struggle with a perception gap. A new product is often a new concept. Users have no frame of reference and can't grasp "this is useful to me" within 30 seconds.

Many teams misdiagnose this as an exposure problem, pouring resources into "getting the word out." But the real bottleneck is: they heard about it — then what? The first step isn't widening the funnel. It's reducing the cost of understanding — making the product's value intuitively obvious on first contact.

Hands-on experience: Grew a content account from 0 to tens of thousands of followers in six months — essentially stress-testing "how to make someone perceive value in 3 seconds" in an information-overloaded environment. What this trained wasn't content tactics, but a sharp instinct for market sentiment: how to make a message spread organically among the right people.
0 → 1
Finding Users Who "Get It" Immediately

Who will be the first to see the value in a new product? Usually people already actively searching for a solution — they have a clear need, have tried existing options, and remain unsatisfied. For them, your product isn't a concept that needs explaining. It's an answer they've been waiting for.

Early growth rarely comes from paid ads. It comes from letting the product itself become the distribution channel. Seed users are both users and channels — their usage naturally generates word-of-mouth.

Hands-on experience: Built a platform's user tagging system and persona taxonomy from scratch, cross-validating behavioral data with user interviews to transform vague "target users" into measurable audience definitions, precisely identifying seed user traits and highest-efficiency acquisition paths.

Replace Intuition with Experiments

Users are coming in, but you changed the landing page, adjusted ad spend, and launched a new feature all at once. Metrics went up — but who gets the credit? Without attribution, there's no replication.

This stage isn't about "doing more." It's about figuring out what actually works. The same budget placed across different channels, audiences, and timing can yield returns that vary by 10x. The key is identifying the real growth levers while avoiding spend on dead-end channels.

Hands-on experience: Built standardized A/B testing frameworks and multi-touch attribution models, identifying the highest-ROI growth paths under constrained budgets. Every growth action has a hypothesis, a control group, and a conclusion — turning "feels effective" into "proven effective."

The core tension: individual capability can't support organizational scale. Growth expertise needs to be embedded in processes and tools so the organization can make data-driven decisions on its own.
