Layer One
The World & I
世 界 与 我

The Dao is like the universe itself — running everything on its own. And that "everything" includes me, you, and everyone.

The seeds between us are interconnected causes and conditions. When I meet someone, it's not because "I" chose to meet them — it's because a seed inherently involves both of us, or even more people.

This is Dependent Origination and Emptiness — there is no independent "I" manufacturing fate. Fate itself is the natural manifestation of the web of causes and conditions.

Seeds are interconnected causes and conditions.
We are merely part of the flow.
Not because "I" chose to meet —
The web of conditions reveals itself naturally.
Layer Two
Events & I
我 与 事 件

For a seed to manifest, causes and conditions must converge. So how does the "cause" happen?

The Dao passes through the right brain, forming what we call "intuition." Those who trust their intuition act on it directly. Those who don't? The brain fabricates a story after the fact, making you believe it was a carefully reasoned decision.

Either way, we followed that "intuition" and triggered the cause.

Meanwhile, the conditions are also brewing — gathering and ripening naturally across space and time. When cause and conditions converge at a single point, an event is born.

Cause: The Dao flows through the right brain, becomes intuition, triggers action.
Conditions: External factors brew and converge on their own.
Convergence: Cause meets conditions — an event is born.

We are not solid entities. We are within the Dao.
Layer Three
I & "I"
我 与 「我」

Originally, all we needed was to go with the flow.

But the sixth consciousness keeps fabricating stories, and the seventh consciousness (Manas) manufactures a fictional "I" — causing us to generate emotions every day: anger, fear, sadness, anxiety… trapping us in an endless loop of stories, thoughts, and worries.

We forgot that we could simply do nothing deliberate. Forgot that we don't need to plan everything. Forgot that we can just let things be. Forgot that everything is already just fine.

Practice is this: when a thought arises, watch it — don't chase it. Once the mind quiets down, we realign with the Dao and flow more smoothly — suddenly remembering: oh, we've been part of this system all along.

I. Thought storm: drowning in emotions and stories daily.
II. Practice: watch thoughts arise, don't chase them.
III. After stillness: flowing along the Dao's path naturally.
