01
Cold Start
Month one: single-digit followers

I'll be honest—when I first started creating content, I gained single-digit followers in the entire first month. Not because I wasn't trying. Quite the opposite: I produced a ton of content that I personally thought was brilliant. Carefully chosen topics, polished copy, beautiful layouts.

Then I hit publish, and it was like tossing a pebble into the ocean. Dead silence.

Eventually I realized: I'd been talking to myself. I wasn't making content users needed—I was making content I wanted to showcase.

If you've ever worked in product, this feeling should ring a bell—you think your feature is amazing, but users couldn't care less. Classic pre-PMF syndrome. Except in content creation, the feedback loop is faster and more brutal: the data doesn't spare your feelings.

02
Listen → Iterate
What the comments section taught me

The turning point came when I started actually reading comments and DMs. Not skimming—reading every single one. What were they complaining about? What were they hoping for? Which phrases made them linger?

I discovered that what users cared about was completely different from what I'd assumed. They didn't care how original your topic was or how deep your take went. What mattered to them was: "Can this thing make me feel just a little bit better in one specific situation I'm stuck in?"

Users aren't "attracted" to you—they resonate with you. You said what they'd been feeling but couldn't articulate. That's the exact moment conversion happens.

So I scrapped my entire content strategy and rebuilt from scratch: instead of starting with "what I want to say," I started with "what they're going through." How to write headlines, how to structure narratives, where to release emotional tension—every decision became a testable hypothesis backed by data.

Within two months, zero to twenty thousand. Not luck—I'd finally learned to listen.

03
Career Impact
How this changed the way I do analysis

Most analysts understand users by starting from data. I have an extra path—I've been the one being "growth-hacked," and I've been the one desperately trying to make people stay.

That means when I see a conversion curve dropping, I'm not just thinking "which funnel stage broke." I'm asking: "What was the user experiencing at that step? What were they thinking? What made them decide to leave?"

Data tells you what happened. Understanding people tells you why—and what to do next.

And because I'm a power user of all kinds of AI tools and content products myself, my sense of "user experience" isn't abstract—it comes with muscle memory. I know whether a feature works well, not because I ran a user interview, but because I got stuck on it yesterday.

04
Growth Framework
The growth playbook I distilled from all of this

Abstract what I learned from content creation one level up, and it's really a user-centered growth loop:

01
Find the real situation
Not a "user persona"—a real person, in a specific context, with an unmet need. Start with that pain point.
02
Find the right angle
The same need can be addressed ten different ways. A great angle makes the user feel "this is about me" in the first second.
03
Ship fast, learn faster
Put it out there, watch the data, read the feedback, adjust. Don't perfect it in your head—let the real world push back.

In content creation, this is called "picking the right topic." In product growth, it's called "running experiments." At the core, it's the same thing: don't get carried away by your own assumptions—find the thing that actually drives people's decisions.
